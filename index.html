Project 02

Reproducing Word2Vec:
CBOW vs. Skip-Gram

Reproduced and compared Word2Vec's two architectures — CBOW and Skip-Gram — evaluating semantic and geometric similarity through embedding visualizations, analogy tasks, and hyperparameter analysis.

🧠
Course
CX 4240 — Comp. Data Analysis
Timeline
Fall 2024
Team Size
4 members
Status
✅ Completed

Overview

One-hot encoding gives words no semantic meaning — there is no way to know that "cat" and "dog" are more related than "cat" and "democracy." Word2Vec solves this by mapping words to dense vectors where similar words cluster together. Our team reproduced Word2Vec from scratch and compared its two architectures: CBOW (predict a target word from surrounding context) and Skip-Gram (predict surrounding context from a target word). Both models use negative sampling for training efficiency.

We evaluated on semantic similarity (Spearman correlation with human judgments) and analogy accuracy — e.g. "King − Man + Woman ≈ ?" — then visualized learned embeddings using t-SNE and cosine similarity heatmaps across 9 model variants.

Model Comparison: CBOW vs. Skip-Gram

CBOW

Direction: Context → Target word

Example: "The _ sits" → "cat"

Strengths: Faster convergence, strong semantic groupings, best Spearman scores (0.40)

Weaknesses: Weaker analogy performance

Skip-Gram

Direction: Target word → Context

Example: "cat" → ["The", "sits"]

Strengths: Better analogy accuracy, captures nuanced and rare-word relationships

Weaknesses: Slower convergence, higher final training loss

Key Contributions

  • Implemented CBOW and Skip-Gram from scratch using negative sampling for efficiency
  • Benchmarked 9 model variants across Spearman semantic similarity and analogy accuracy
  • Systematically varied negative samples (neg=10), window size (win=10), and embedding dimension (dim=200)
  • Generated t-SNE projections and pairwise cosine similarity heatmaps to interpret geometric structure
  • Found dim=200 led to overfitting; CBOW-neg10 achieved best overall Spearman score of 0.40

Hyperparameter Findings

  • Negative Sampling (neg=10): Boosted Skip-Gram analogy accuracy but reduced CBOW performance
  • Window Size (win=10): Slight Skip-Gram improvement by capturing longer-range context
  • Embedding Dimension (dim=200): Caused overfitting in both models — no meaningful gain over dim=100
  • Best Spearman: CBOW-base and CBOW-neg10 both hit 0.40 — highest across all 9 variants
  • Best Analogy Accuracy: SG-neg10 and SG-win10 achieved 0.21, confirming Skip-Gram's niche advantage for analogies

Takeaways

The right architecture depends on the task. CBOW is faster and better at grouping semantically similar words — suitable for most general NLP applications. Skip-Gram excels at analogies and understanding rare or contextually nuanced words. Modern approaches like GloVe and BERT build on these same foundations, but Word2Vec remains a key conceptual stepping stone in understanding how language models learn meaning from raw text.
